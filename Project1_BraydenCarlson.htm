Question List

Use the following prompts to evaluate the selected web site:

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information?
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Do the graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background was used, did it help or hurt?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was it well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look & feel repeated on all pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Trader Joe’s – https://www.traderjoes.com/

Reviewed on September 10, 2025 using a laptop browser (desktop viewport) and a phone (mobile viewport).

  1. Q1 – First impression: Friendly, upbeat, and approachable. The homepage highlights seasonal items with large, high-quality images and playful illustrations that match the brand’s personality. The overall feel is welcoming and informal rather than corporate.
  2. Q2 – Information flow: Primary navigation appears at the top with clear categories (e.g., Products, Recipes, Stores, Podcasts/News). Below that, the homepage uses hero sections and card-style tiles to route you to featured content. Calls-to-action are short and obvious.
  3. Q3 – Site structure sense: The structure is logical: top-level categories lead to browsable listings with filters; detail pages use consistent headings, images, and ingredient or description blocks. Breadcrumbs and “Back to results” links reduce getting lost.
  4. Q4 – Graphics/layout/multimedia: Art direction is consistent with Trader Joe’s in-store vibe (hand-drawn accents and bright product shots). Graphics enhance scannability and do not overwhelm text. Multimedia is lightweight and complements the content.
  5. Q5 – Load time: Pages are image-heavy but optimized; on a typical broadband connection they load quickly and remain responsive after initial paint. Caching appears effective when navigating between product and recipe pages.
  6. Q6 – Readability & backgrounds: Text contrasts well against light backgrounds, with generous font sizing and spacing. Body text is easy to read; headings are bold without being overpowering. Backgrounds are mostly white or light neutral tones that keep focus on content.
  7. Q7 – Navigation quality: The sticky top navigation and prominent search box make it easy to jump between tasks (finding a product, a recipe, or a nearby store). Footer navigation mirrors key links for redundancy. Overall, navigation is clear and consistent.
  8. Q8 – Consistent look & feel: The brand’s red accents, hand-lettered style headings, and illustration style repeat across pages. Buttons, cards, and spacing follow a consistent visual system which helps recognition and speeds up browsing.
  9. Q9 – Responsive design: On smaller screens, the navigation collapses into a mobile menu. Cards stack vertically, images resize, and interactive elements remain finger-friendly with adequate touch targets. Content remains complete without awkward horizontal scrolling.
  10. Q10 – Notable strengths: The integrated recipe ecosystem (linking products to recipes), the personable editorial tone (e.g., “Fearless Flyer”-style content), and the store locator together create a shopping-plus-inspiration experience that goes beyond a basic grocery catalog.
